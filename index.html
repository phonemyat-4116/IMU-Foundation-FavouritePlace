Japanese Education

Japanese education is highly respected and considered to be one of the best in the world. The education system in Japan is highly centralized and is primarily overseen by the Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology.


Education in Japan is compulsory for children between the ages of 6 and 15. After completing compulsory education, students have the option to attend either upper secondary school, which is similar to high school in other countries, or vocational school, which provides training in a specific trade or skill.


The curriculum in Japanese schools focuses on a wide range of subjects, including math, science, Japanese language, social studies, and physical education. In addition, many schools offer extracurricular activities, such as sports, music, and art.


One notable feature of Japanese education is the emphasis on discipline and hard work. Students are expected to be punctual, respectful, and diligent in their studies. The school day is often long, and many students attend after-school tutoring or cram schools to prepare for exams.
